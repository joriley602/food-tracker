⚠️ Heart & Metabolic Watch-Outs

Three things worth knowing about — even if you don't track them daily.
Saturated Fat
Heart Foundation target: ≤20g/day (≈10% of calories at 1,800 cal). Raises LDL cholesterol; matters more after 60 as arteries stiffen.

Watch out for: fatty cuts of red meat, full-fat cheese (10g sat fat per 30g slice isn't unusual), butter, cream, pastries and biscuits, coconut oil, processed meats (bacon, sausages, salami), takeaway fried foods.

Better swaps: chicken/fish/kangaroo over fatty red meat · olive oil over butter · Greek yoghurt over cream · nuts over biscuits · grill or bake over fry.
Sugar (especially added)
WHO target: ≤50g/day total sugar (≈10% of calories), ideally ≤25g/day added sugar. Drives visceral fat, insulin resistance, and blood pressure.

Watch out for: soft drinks (40g in one can), fruit juice (same as soft drink for sugar), flavoured yoghurts (often 20g+), muesli and "healthy" cereals (check the label — some are 25% sugar), sauces (tomato/BBQ/sweet chilli), muesli bars, dried fruit, Asian stir-fry sauces, breakfast pastries.

Better swaps: water or unsweetened tea over juice/soft drink · plain Greek yoghurt + berries over flavoured · whole fruit over dried fruit · read labels (under 5g/100g is low, over 15g/100g is high).
Sodium (salt)
Heart Foundation target: ≤2,000mg/day (≈5g salt / 1 tsp). Aussies average 3,400mg — most from processed foods, not the salt shaker. Directly affects blood pressure, which matters more after 60.

Watch out for: bread (170mg/slice × 4 slices = 680mg before toppings), bacon & ham (1,000mg+ per serve), cheese (200mg per 30g slice), canned soup (800mg+ per bowl), baked beans (700mg per tin), pasta sauce in jars, soy sauce (900mg per tablespoon), olives, stock cubes, restaurant/takeaway meals (often 1,500mg+ per meal), Vegemite.

Better swaps: look for "low salt" bread (under 400mg/100g) · fresh meat over deli/processed · rinse canned beans (cuts sodium ~40%) · herbs, lemon, pepper, garlic over salt · cook at home more · read labels (under 120mg/100g is low, over 600mg/100g is high).
💡 The 80/20 principle
If 80% of what you eat is whole foods (meat, fish, eggs, vegetables, fruit, legumes, plain dairy, wholegrains, nuts), all three of these sort themselves out naturally. The overwhelming majority of saturated fat, added sugar, and sodium in the average Aussie diet comes from the 20% that's processed. You don't need to track these daily — you just need to know which specific processed items you eat regularly, and whether those items are the hidden culprits. Read the label on anything that comes in a packet once, and you'll know forever.

💡 Quick Tips for Hitting Your Targets

Protein under by dinner? Add a protein shake with milk (39g P) or 200g Greek yoghurt (20g P).

Fibre under? Half a cup of lentils or black beans added to any meal = 8g fibre instantly.

Carbs feel low-energy? Switch white rice/bread for brown rice, quinoa, or wholegrain — same calories, 3–5x the fibre.

Belly fat focus: Soluble fibre (oats, beans, lentils, apples, pears) specifically targets visceral fat. Aim for 10g+ of your 30g from these sources.

Snack attack? Most "hungry between meals" is actually thirst or boredom. Glass of water first, wait 10 minutes — if still hungry, pick from the snacks list above rather than whatever's in the cupboard.
